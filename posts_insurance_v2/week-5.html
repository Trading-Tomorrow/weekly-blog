Week 5

Agent Communication & Webots ROS2 Integration

2 min read · by Team

During the fifth week, we advanced our project development significantly, shifting focus towards implementation details for both multi-agent communication and robotics simulation:

Our key progress and structural decisions include:

  • Multi-Agent Architecture Redesign: We refactored agent communication by altering the initial system architecture. We decided to remove the facilitator agent because it was taking on too many responsibilities, creating a bottleneck. Removing the facilitator allows direct agent-to-agent communication, encouraging more flexible and decentralized interactions.
  • Robotics & Webots Simulation: We successfully imported the environment map and configured our humanoid robot inside Webots. Our focus is now on developing the bridge and communication pipelines between the Webots simulation and ROS2 to enable control and sensor data acquisition.
  • Team Dynamics & Collaboration: We also began experiencing some collaboration challenges this week. It has felt like the group is not functioning with the cohesion and output expected of a 6-person team, highlighting the need to adjust how we coordinate and distribute tasks going forward.
These changes streamline our system architecture, progress our simulation setup, and highlight areas where we need to strengthen our teamwork.
